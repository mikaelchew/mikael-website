# Marblism Social Media Content Generation — Complete Brand Brief

## BRAND IDENTITY

### Who Is Mikael Chew?
Mikael Chew is a direct selling industry strategist with 23+ years of experience. He spent 8 years building in the field (prospecting, team building, frontline leadership) and 15 years in corporate management at 3 multinational direct selling companies. He is the author of an upcoming book applying Sun Tzu's Art of War to modern direct selling strategy.

### Brand Statement
"I help direct selling leaders stop hustling and start thinking strategically — to build effective teams with lasting success. Because in this industry, the right strategy always beats the hardest hustle."

### Core Philosophy
- **Win the Right Way** — Ethics is a competitive advantage, not a constraint
- **Strategy Over Hustle** — Hard work without direction is just exhaustion
- **Build Leaders, Not Followers** — True success is measured by how many people no longer need you

### Target Audience
Mid-to-senior direct selling / network marketing leaders who:
- Have been in the industry 2+ years
- Feel stuck at their current level
- Are tired of "hustle harder" advice and want real strategic frameworks
- Believe in building with integrity
- Want mentorship from someone who has real experience, not just motivation

### Brand Voice & Tone
- **Authoritative but approachable** — speaks with earned credibility, never arrogant
- **Strategic and precise** — uses frameworks, models, and structured thinking
- **Story-driven** — illustrates points with real-world examples and specific people/numbers
- **Anti-hype and contrarian** — challenges industry orthodoxy directly. NEVER uses hype language
- **Honest and direct** — does not sugarcoat. Tells uncomfortable truths when needed
- **Teaching mindset** — always gives actionable insight, not just observations
- **Military/strategic metaphors** — uses language of generals, terrain, battles, alliances (drawn from Sun Tzu)

### CRITICAL VOICE RULES — DO NOT VIOLATE
- NEVER use hype language: "crushing it," "grind," "hustle harder," "boss babe," "freedom lifestyle," "passive income," "unlimited potential," "change your life overnight"
- NEVER use MLM cliches: "this business changed my life," "join my team," "DM me for details," "who's ready to level up?"
- NEVER promise quick results or easy money
- NEVER be vague or motivational without substance — every post must contain a specific insight, framework, or actionable lesson
- NEVER use excessive emojis (maximum 1-2 per post, and only where natural)
- NEVER use exclamation marks excessively (maximum 1 per post)
- The tone is a STRATEGIC ADVISOR speaking to peers, not a motivational speaker hyping a crowd

### Language
- ALL content in English
- No Chinese language content at this stage

**Content distribution across pillars**:
- Monday: Strategic Frameworks (educational, sets the tone for the week)

**Format guidelines**:
- Text posts: 1,200-2,000 characters. Start with a strong hook (first 2 lines must stop the scroll). Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences each). End with a question or call to reflect.
- Carousel posts: 7-10 slides. Title slide + content slides + CTA slide. Each slide has ONE key point.
- Image posts: Quote graphics or framework diagrams with clean brand visuals.

**LinkedIn-specific tone**: More professional and analytical. Use data points and industry observations. Reference business strategy concepts. This is where Mikael speaks as a peer to other leaders and industry professionals.

**CTA patterns (rotate)**:
- "What's your experience with this? Share in the comments."
- "Agree or disagree? I want to hear your perspective."
- "Save this for the next time you face this challenge."
- "Follow for more strategic direct selling insights."
- "Read the full article: www.mikaelchew.com/blog/[slug]"

**Hashtag strategy** (3-5 per post):

### Content Multiplication Method
For each blog article, generate the following variations:

1. **The Hook Post**: Take the single most provocative line and make it a standalone post (Twitter, IG quote graphic)
2. **The Framework Post**: Extract the actionable framework/model into a carousel or numbered list (LinkedIn, IG carousel)
3. **The Story Post**: Retell the personal story or case study in first person (Facebook, LinkedIn)
4. **The Contrarian Post**: Frame the core argument as a challenge to conventional wisdom (Twitter, LinkedIn)
5. **The Teaching Post**: Break down the "how to" into 5-7 actionable steps (IG carousel, Facebook)
6. **The Question Post**: Turn the article's thesis into an open-ended question for engagement (all platforms)
7. **The Sun Tzu Connection**: Link the article's theme to a specific Sun Tzu principle (IG quote, Twitter)

That gives 7 unique content angles per blog article x 14 articles = **98 unique content pieces**, each adapted for 4 platforms = **392 platform-specific posts**.

## WHAT TO ABSOLUTELY AVOID

1. **No company-specific promotion** in thought leadership content (no Zinzino mentions unless explicitly a recruitment post)
2. **No income claims** or screenshots of earnings
3. **No before/after photos** or transformation testimonials that imply guaranteed results
4. **No "DM me" culture** — never end a post with "DM me to learn more"
5. **No hype language** (see critical voice rules above)
6. **No engagement bait** ("Comment YES if you agree!")
7. **No generic motivational quotes** from other people (only Sun Tzu quotes with original interpretation, or Mikael's own words)
8. **No busy, cluttered, or off-brand visuals** — stick strictly to the color palette
9. **No trending audio/memes** that don't align with the strategic, authoritative tone
10. **No negative talk about competitors, other companies, or other leaders**

Track these metrics monthly:

- Top-performing format (carousel vs. text vs. quote graphic)

Optimize by doubling down on what works and reducing what doesn't. Adjust pillar distribution based on engagement data.
